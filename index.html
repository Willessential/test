Understanding Power Distribution and Backfeed

Ensuring Safety in Our Power Networks

Introduction to Power Distribution and Its Challenges

In our role as an Electrical Distribution company, we oversee the vast network of powerlines across the state. We serve as the main hub that enables residences and businesses to exchange electricity, facilitating not just the distribution, but also the exchange of energy. However, in this complex and ever-evolving landscape, there are challenges that could affect our operations and the safety of our staff. One such issue is the phenomenon known as backfeed.

Unpacking the Concept: Backfeed Phenomenon

Overview of Backfeed

Backfeed refers to a situation where electricity, instead of following its usual path from the power station to the customer, reverses its direction. This can occur when an electrical generator such as solar panels or a diesel generator is not wired correctly, feeding power back into the electrical grid.

Risks Associated with Backfeed

Backfeed can prove hazardous for our employees. If the backfeed incident occurs while they are working on the power lines, the lines can become unexpectedly energised. Despite adhering to deenergisation procedures, our linesmen may be unwittingly exposed to these energised lines, facing possible electrocution or other related injuries.

Understanding Solar and Generation Technologies and Their Relation to Backfeed

Navigating through the Latest Generation Technologies

As residential and commercial sectors increasingly adopt advanced electrical generation technologies such as solar panels and diesel generators, the risk of backfeed increases. These technologies, when improperly installed or mismanaged, can become sources of backfeed accidents.

Spotting Potential Backfeed Issues

It is crucial that our teams are trained to recognise potential backfeed issues. Looking out for signs such as persisting voltage on the lines, even after disconnection, can provide early warnings of a potential backfeed situation.
